Scoop by Evelyn Waugh

Scoop is commonly regarded as a satire of journalism. Published in the 1930s, with war looming perhaps it was a satire of social mismanagement altogether as a mistaken identity sends a country life writer to Africa.

The supporting characters are better than the main ones, the Boot cousins who represent the archtypes of city (John) and country (William) in England.

At a banquet given in his honour Sir Jocelyn Hitchcock once modestly attributed his success in life to the habit of "getting up earlier than the other fellow." But this was partly metaphorical, partly false and in case wholly relative for journalists are as a rule late risers.

The incapable country cousin, sent to an African country to cover revolution, is a hit when all the competition is sidelined. He returns to England triumphant but ready to return to the country beat.
