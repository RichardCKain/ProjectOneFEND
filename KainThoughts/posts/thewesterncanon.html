The Western Canon by Harold Bloom

Bloom gives a whirlwind tour of Western Literature. Most of the usual suspects are covered (Milton, Coleridge, Whitman).

Alfred North Whitehead said the history of Western Philosophy is a series of commentaries on Plato. Bloom goes a step further with his central figure, Shakespeare.

Shakespeare is at the center of the Canon at least in part because Hamlet is. The introspective consciousness, free to contemplate itself, remains the most elitist of all Western images, but without it the Canon is not possible, and, to put it most bluently, neither are we.

Cervantes was a contemporary of Shakespeare but there is little evidence they read one another.
